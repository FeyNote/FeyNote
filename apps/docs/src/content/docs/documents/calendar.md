The calendar feature lets you keep track of events in your campaign — whether that's managing sessions, building a custom world calendar, or tracking your party's timeline. This feature is currently in alpha, with more updates and improvements on the way!

## Creating a Calendar

Create a calendar by selecting the Create Calendar button. Like any other document in Feynote, calendars can be shared with your group.

### Customizing a Calendar

Once created, open the calendar settings with the ⚙ icon in the top-right corner. You'll find three layout options:

- **Gregorian** — The standard real-world calendar, great for keeping things familiar.
- **Session** — A numbered list format that lets you link notes to individual sessions.
- **Custom** — Build a calendar from scratch tailored to your world. Define the names of days and months, how many days are in a week, how many weeks to a month, and how many months make up a year.

## Creating a Calendar Event

Calendar events are created directly from your text documents using the @ symbol. Select the calendar you'd like to reference, and a popover will appear letting you choose the date you want that document to appear on. Once linked, the document's title will show up on the calendar on that date. Both the calendar entry and the in-document reference are clickable and will take you straight to the linked document!

## How People Use the Calendar

### Session Management

The session calendar is popular with players who organize their notes by session. Tag each document with its session number to see your party's journey unfold from one session to the next — especially useful when paired with notes about locations or NPCs.

### Timeline Management

For DMs and players alike, the calendar is a powerful tool for tracking in-world events. DMs can log events like "Fall of Helmsreach" or upcoming plot beats, while players can mark their current in-game date and keep tabs on promises, deadlines, or looming consequences.
