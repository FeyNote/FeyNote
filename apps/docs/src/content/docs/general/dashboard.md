title: Dashboard
description: Your home screen in FeyNote, with quick access to recent documents, the document graph, AI threads, and shared content.
---

The dashboard is the first thing you see when you open FeyNote for the first time. It gives you a quick overview of your collection through a set of cards, each highlighting a different slice of your content. You can get to the dashboard at any time by clicking **Dashboard** in the left sidebar. If you have a [workspace](/docs/general/workspaces) active, the dashboard shows content from that workspace only. Switch to **Everything** in the workspace picker to see content across all workspaces.

## Recent Documents

The **Recent Documents** card shows your 10 most recently updated documents, sorted by the last time each one was changed. Click any document in the list to open it. If you want to see your full history, click the expand button in the top-right corner of the card to open the complete recent documents list.

If you haven't created any documents yet, this card will show an empty state prompting you to create your first one.

## Graph

The **Graph** card shows an embedded preview of your [document graph](/docs/documents/graph), giving you a bird's-eye view of your documents and the connections between them. Click the expand button to open the full graph view, where you can interact with nodes, adjust settings, and explore your collection visually.

## Recent Threads

The **Recent Threads** card lists your 10 most recently updated AI assistant threads. Click any thread to pick up the conversation where you left off, or click the expand button to browse all of your threads.

This card only appears once you have created at least one thread. If you haven't used the AI assistant yet, you won't see it on your dashboard.

## Shared With You

The **Shared With You** card shows up to 10 documents that other users have shared with you, sorted by most recently updated. Click any document to open it, or click the expand button to see the full list of shared content.

For more on how sharing works, see [Sharing Documents](/docs/documents/sharing).
